Legal fix
"5-year initial term" → "10-year initial term"
The original deck stated "5-year initial term with renewal options." The Franchise Agreement (FA) Section 3.1 specifies a 10-year initial term. This is a direct factual contradiction between the marketing material and the binding legal agreement — if a prospect relied on the 5-year figure when signing a 10-year contract, it creates significant legal exposure.
Original
"5-year initial term with renewal options and clear exit policies discussed after NDA"
New version
"10-year initial term with renewal option. Full terms are provided in the Franchise Disclosure Document (FDD)."
Preeti's Critical Concerns Preeti's document explicitly flags this: "FA Section 3.1 provides a 10-year initial term (expiring the day before the 10th anniversary of store opening)" and recommends revising to "10-Year Initial Term with Renewal Option."
Softened
"2–3 months from signing to grand opening" → variability-aware language
The original stated this as a hard commitment. The FDD contains no such timeline guarantee. Third-party dependencies — permitting, landlord approvals, construction — routinely extend Canadian retail buildouts to 3–6+ months. A hard promise that isn't kept creates disputes.
Original
"2-3 months from signing to grand opening4fast, efficient, and stress-free launch process"
New version
"A structured launch process from signing to opening. Timelines vary based on site selection, permitting, construction, and landlord approvals."
Preeti's Critical Concerns Preeti's document notes no specific timeline guarantee is present in the FDD and recommends language that acknowledges variability.

Legal fix
"Strong Returns, Proven Performance" title removed
The FDD explicitly avoids earnings representations. The phrase "proven performance" combined with a revenue/profit chart constitutes an implied earnings representation. If a franchisee loses money and points to this slide, it creates liability.
Original
"Financial Model: Strong Returns, Proven Performance"
New version
"The investment model"
Preeti's Critical Concerns Memo recommends changing to neutral wording like "Scalable Retail Model" or removing earnings-implying language from the title entirely.
Removed
Revenue/profit/cost bar chart replaced
The original showed bars for Average Revenue, Net Profit, and Operating Costs "based on current operating locations" with no time period, no store count, no source, and no clarification of whether figures are actual or projected. This is an unauthorized earnings representation under Canadian franchise law. Replaced with an investment-allocation donut chart — which describes cost structure, not earnings.
Preeti's Critical Concerns Memo explicitly states: "chart lacks any explanation of data source, store count, time period, or whether it reflects actual vs projected performance" and recommends only using such charts if supported by a proper Financial Performance Representation in the FDD.
Added
Financial disclaimer added (must print)
Added a mandatory disclaimer on the financials slide that prints in the PDF: "Figures are illustrative and based on a limited number of operating locations. They are not a guarantee of revenue, profit, or individual results. This document does not constitute a financial performance representation. Please refer to the FDD for complete information."
